## Recognition Guidelines

### Inclusion Criteria

To be included in the Hall of Fame, researchers must:
- ✅ Report a valid security vulnerability
- ✅ Follow responsible disclosure process
- ✅ Provide sufficient detail and proof of concept
- ✅ Allow reasonable time for remediation
- ✅ Consent to public recognition

### Recognition Format

Each entry includes:
- **Researcher Name/Handle**: As preferred by researcher
- **Vulnerability**: Brief description (non-technical)
- **Severity**: Critical/High/Medium/Low
- **Date**: Month and year of disclosure
- **Bounty**: Amount paid (if researcher consents)

### Privacy Options

Researchers may choose to:
- Use their real name
- Use a pseudonym/handle
- Remain anonymous (not listed)
- Decline bounty amount disclosure

---

## Entry Template

When a vulnerability is disclosed, entries follow this format:

```markdown
### [Researcher Name/Handle]

**Vulnerability**: [Brief non-technical description]  
**Severity**: [Critical/High/Medium/Low]  
**Disclosed**: [Month Year]  
**Bounty**: [Amount or "Undisclosed"]  

[Optional: Brief thank you message or additional context]

**Links**:
- [Security Advisory](link)
- [Researcher Profile](link) (if applicable)
```

## How to Get Listed

1. **Discover a Vulnerability**: Find a security issue in StellarSwipe
2. **Report Responsibly**: Follow our disclosure policy in `SECURITY.md`
3. **Cooperate**: Work with us during verification and remediation
4. **Wait for Disclosure**: Allow coordinated public disclosure
5. **Get Recognized**: You'll be added to this page upon disclosure

**Ready to contribute?** Read our [Security Policy](../../SECURITY.md) and [Researcher Resources](researcher_resources.md) to get started!
